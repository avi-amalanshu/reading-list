# Gem Alert
Here is a list of manuscripts (papers/articles/monographs/tutorials etc.) that I find myself referring to in an unexpectedly diverse variety of contexts. 
## Probabilistic Modelling and Variational Inference
1. Albergo, Boffi, Vanden-Eijden: [Stochastic Interpolants: A Unifying Framework for Flows and Diffusions.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.08797) ([official impl](https://github.com/malbergo/stochastic-interpolants)) *rigorous, unifying analysis and generalization of continous-time stochastic interpolation*
2. McAllester, Stratos: [Formal Limitations on the Measurement of Mutual Information](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1811.04251) ([official impl](https://github.com/karlstratos/doe)) *fundamental KL and MI estimation bounds, interesting proof technique*
3. Krueger et al.: [Bayesian Hypernetworks](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1710.04759) *very flexible and underexplored Bayesian model*
4. Touchette: [The large deviation approach to statistical mechanics](https://terpconnect.umd.edu/~cjarzyns/CHEM-CHPH-PHYS_703_Spr_20/resources/Touchette-2009.pdf) *digestible bridge to statistical mechanics for people with ml bg*
5. Luo: [Understanding Diﬀusion Models: A Unified Perspective](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2208.11970) *have only skimmed through this, but recommended reading imo*
6. Zhi-Han [Training Latent Variable Models with Auto-encoding Variational Bayes: A Tutorial](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2208.07818) *intuitive exposition of AEVB as approximate EM*
7. Marion et al.: [Implicit Diffusion: Efficient Optimization through Stochastic Sampling](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.05468v3) ([official impl](https://github.com/google-deepmind/implicit_diffusion)) *optimize implicit models from samples/without going through likelihood estimation*
## Nonparametric and Semiparametric Inference
1. Chernozhukov et al.: [Automatic Debiased Machine Learning via Riesz Regression](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2104.14737) ([official impl for a follow-up work](https://github.com/victor5as/RieszLearning)) *foundational for AutoDML*
2. Foster and Syrgkanis: [Orthogonal Statistical Learning](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1901.09036) ([official impl](https://github.com/syrgkanislab/orthogonal_learning)) *analysis like AutoDML outside the standard M/Z-estimand form*
3. Tibshirani: [Conformal Prediction](https://www.stat.berkeley.edu/~ryantibs/statlearn-s23/lectures/conformal.pdf) *gentle and balanced introduction to conformal prediction*
4. Han et al.: [Optimal rates of entropy estimation over Lipschitz balls](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1711.02141) *synthesis of various ways of using simple polynomial approximations for estimating functionals*
## Representation Learning
1. Zhai et al.: [Contextures: Representations from Contexts](https://openreview.net/pdf?id=4GZwFPzLgW) *fundamental work in our understanding of representation learning + scaling laws, math is a little terse though*
2. Tsai, Yeh, Ravikumar: [Faith-Shap: The Faithful Shapley Interaction Index](https://jmlr.org/papers/volume24/22-0202/22-0202.pdf) ([official impl](https://github.com/chepingt/Faith-Shap)) *feature importance w/o unhealthy assumptions*
## Misc.
1. Lin: [Bayesian Epistemology](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/epistemology-bayesian/#ClosExpaTerrBaye) *scratches a certain itch*
